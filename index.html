1. The Andean Condor

The Andean Condor is a national symbol of Colombia and one of the world’s largest flying birds. You should admire its wingspan and learn about its importance for Andean culture and biodiversity.

2. El Cocuy National Park

You should explore El Cocuy, a protected area with snow-capped mountains, glaciers, and páramos. Hiking here would give you an unforgettable adventure in Colombia’s highlands.

3. Cocora Valley

In Cocora Valley, you should walk among the world’s tallest palm trees. The landscape is perfect for ecotourism and photography, and it could be the highlight of your journey.

4. Villa de Leyva

This colonial town offers cobblestone streets and white houses. You could visit its main square, museums, and nearby archaeological sites that connect culture with history.

5. San Agustín Archaeological Park

In San Agustín, you should take a guided tour to discover ancient statues and sacred places. A guide would explain the meaning of each monument and the traditions of indigenous peoples.
